Hello,

Here's a common scenario - 'Clients come to us with an idea for a product and they always ask the questions - how long will it take and how much will it cost us to deliver? And management in turn has to plan in order to make sure that they have the financial means and the resources for all the projects the company has.'

High-level planning takes care of budget estimates and delivery times but, it does not include detailed project plans – these will be developed later. High-level plans need to be defined with built-in recognition and embracement of the variability and the randomness inherent in every project. We can’t control the Waves of Uncertainty, but we can learn How to Surf!
