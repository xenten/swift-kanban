Probabilistic approach for preparing high-level project plans
 
Dimitar Bakardzhiev | 10AM PDT, June 17, 2014
 
Here's a common scenario - 'Clients come to us with an idea for a product and they ask how long it will take and how much it will cost to deliver?'
 
High-level planning takes care of budget estimates and delivery times. We donâ€™t plan in detail what is not absolutely necessary to plan. So, the plan is created with appropriate buffers protecting the decisions that have to be included in the plan. High-level plans need to be defined with built-in recognition and embracement of the variability and the randomness inherent in every project.
 
What will you learn in this webinar?
1) You will get an introduction to a probabilistic approach for preparing high-level plans.
2) You will also learn how to use Monte Carlo simulation for predicting the delivery time for your next project.

The method presented can be used by any team that uses user stories for planning and tracking project execution no matter the development process used (Scrum, XP, Kanban systems).
